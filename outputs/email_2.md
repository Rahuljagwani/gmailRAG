# Email 2: Orthodontist coverage for my daughter

**From:** r.chen@company.com

**Body:**

> My daughter needs braces and her orthodontist isn't in our dental network. What's the lifetime maximum the plan will pay for orthodontic care if we go out-of-network vs. staying in-network? Trying to figure out how much we'll be on the hook for.

**has_clear_answer:** `True`

## Drafted answer

Hi there,

Your plan's orthodontic lifetime maximums depend on which providers you choose. Here's the breakdown under the current plan (effective January 1, 2022):

**Network (in-network orthodontist):** The plan will pay up to $2,000 in lifetime benefits, covering 50 percent of contracted fees.

**Out-of-network:** The plan will pay up to $1,000 in lifetime benefits, covering 50 percent of Delta Dental's allowance.

One important thing to note: The two pools are combined. Any benefits you receive from out-of-network care count toward your out-of-network maximum, but they also count toward your total network maximum. For example, if your daughter receives $500 in out-of-network orthodontic benefits, she could still qualify for up to $1,500 more from the network coverage (not the full $2,000), bringing the total plan payment to $2,000.

Since your daughter's orthodontist is out-of-network, it's a good idea to ask the orthodontist to request a predetermination of benefits from Delta Dental before treatment starts. That way you'll know exactly what your out-of-pocket costs will be.

Best regards,
Benefits Team
