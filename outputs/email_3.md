# Email 3: Travel costs for a specialist out of state

**From:** dpatel@company.com

**Body:**

> My doctor is referring me to a specialist for a procedure that isn't available anywhere near me. Does our medical plan cover any of the travel or hotel costs to go get this done out of state? Not sure if this is something the plan even considers.

**has_clear_answer:** `True`

## Drafted answer

Hi there,

Good news – yes, your Chevron Medical PPO Plan does cover travel and lodging costs for out-of-state specialist care. "This plan provision reimburses you for charges incurred for reasonable, qualifying travel expenses that are essential to receive any covered medical service under the Chevron Medical PPO Plan."

Your situation appears to qualify. The benefit applies when "the covered service is not reasonably available to you from a network provider or facility within 100 miles of your home residence" – which matches your scenario if the procedure truly isn't available nearby.

Qualifying reimbursable expenses include:
- Mileage for your personal car
- Rental cars
- Airline or train tickets
- Hotel lodging (subject to IRS per diem limits)
- Parking fees, tolls, and ride-share services

Important points: The travel benefit does not count toward your deductible, and the specialist's procedure itself will continue to be covered under the plan's normal medical benefits. Meals and personal items are not covered. You'll need to file a travel claim with documentation no later than six months after the year in which the service is provided.

I'd recommend contacting the plan administrator before your trip to confirm the procedure is a covered medical service and to understand any specific requirements or preauthorization needs.

Best regards,
Benefits Team
